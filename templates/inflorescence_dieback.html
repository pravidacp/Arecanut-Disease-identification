Inflorescence Die Back Disease Details

Disease Symptoms:

  • The disease appears on the rachillae of the male flowers and then in the inflorescence dies back the main rachis as brownish patches which soon spread from tip downwards covering the entire rachis and causing wilting.
  • The female flowers of the infected rachis shed and the whole inflorescence shows ‘die back’ symptoms.
  • The fruiting bodies of the fungus (conidia) appear as concentric rings in the discolored areas.
  • The disease is severe mostly during dry conditions (February-March). Button shedding followed by die-back of the inflorescence is a severe problem in areca nut plantations during monsoon periods.

Management

  • Field sanitation practices including the removal and burning of infected dried bunches to reduce the inoculum level in the garden should be strictly followed.
  • Spraying of mancozeb (3 gil) while opening female flowers will reduce the disease incidence. A second spraying should be taken up after 20-25 days.
